# Awesome Korean Learning [![Awesome](https://awesome.re/badge.svg)](https://awesome.re)

> **"I studied Korean for 2 years. Then I watched a drama without subtitles and understood nothing."**

Sound familiar? Every Korean learner hits this wall. Textbook Korean and real Korean are two different languages.

The gap isn't vocabulary. It's **context**. The same word — 괜찮아요 — means "don't worry about it" in one tone, "I can handle it" in another, and "I'm hurt but I won't say it" in a third. No textbook teaches that. But [hearing native speakers use it](https://tubelang.com/korean-expressions/en/gwaenchanayo) makes it instantly obvious.

This list curates the **best free resources** for learning Korean — from absolute beginner to understanding K-dramas without subtitles.

## Context-Based Learning

> The fastest path from "textbook Korean" to "real Korean"

- [Tubelang](https://tubelang.com) — Search any Korean expression and see it in real YouTube clips by native speakers. See how 그냥 has [5+ meanings](https://tubelang.com/korean-expressions/en/geunyang), why [눈치](https://tubelang.com/korean-expressions/en/nunji) has no English equivalent, and what [서운해요](https://tubelang.com/korean-expressions/en/seounhaeyo) actually feels like. Free.
- [Tubelang Expression Guides](https://tubelang.com/korean-expressions/en) — Deep dives into 11 commonly misunderstood expressions with video examples.

## Courses & Grammar

- [Talk to Me in Korean](https://talktomeinkorean.com) — The gold standard for Korean grammar. Levels 1-9. Free tier.
- [How to Study Korean](https://www.howtostudykorean.com) — Free, comprehensive grammar from Unit 0 to advanced.
- [Coursera: First Step Korean](https://www.coursera.org/learn/learn-korean) — Free Yonsei University course.
- [King Sejong Institute](https://www.iksi.or.kr) — Korean government-sponsored courses worldwide.
- [TOPIK Guide](https://www.topikguide.com) — TOPIK test preparation.

## YouTube Channels

- [Talk to Me in Korean](https://www.youtube.com/@talktomeinkorean) — Grammar + culture.
- [Go Billy Korean](https://www.youtube.com/@GoBillyKorean) — Natural-speed explanations. Zero production value. Pure learning.
- [Korean Unnie](https://www.youtube.com/@KoreanUnnie) — Practical expressions + cultural context.
- [Conversational Korean](https://www.youtube.com/@ConversationalKorean) — Real conversations for listening.
- [Prof Yoon](https://www.youtube.com/@ProfYoon) — Academic Korean instruction.

## Podcasts

- [TTMIK Podcast](https://talktomeinkorean.com/audio) — Multi-level conversational Korean.
- [TTMIK Iyagi](https://talktomeinkorean.com/curriculum/iyagi-series/) — Natural conversations (intermediate+).

## Dictionaries

- [Naver Dictionary](https://dict.naver.com) — The most comprehensive Korean-English dictionary.
- [National Institute of Korean Language](https://krdict.korean.go.kr) — Official Korean dictionary.
- [Papago](https://papago.naver.com) — Korean-optimized translation (better than Google for Korean).
- [Forvo](https://forvo.com/languages/ko/) — Native speaker pronunciation database.

- [Memrise](https://www.memrise.com) — Vocabulary with native speaker clips.
