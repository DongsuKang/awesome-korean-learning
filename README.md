## Web Apps & Tools

- [Tubelang](https://tubelang.com) — Search Korean expressions and see them in real YouTube clips by native speakers. Free.
- [Mirinae](https://mirinae.io) — Korean sentence analyzer with grammar breakdown.
- [Papago](https://papago.naver.com) — Korean-optimized translation (better than Google for Korean).
- [Forvo](https://forvo.com/languages/ko/) — Native speaker pronunciation database.
- [Sejong Korean](https://www.sejonghakdang.org) — Official Korean government learning platform.

## Courses & Structured Learning

- [Talk to Me in Korean](https://talktomeinkorean.com) — The gold standard for Korean grammar. Free tier available.
- [KoreanClass101](https://www.koreanclass101.com) — Audio/video lessons with structured paths.
- [Coursera: First Step Korean](https://www.coursera.org/learn/learn-korean) — Free Yonsei University course.
- [How to Study Korean](https://www.howtostudykorean.com) — Free comprehensive grammar lessons.
- [King Sejong Institute](https://www.iksi.or.kr) — Korean government-sponsored courses worldwide.
- [TOPIK Guide](https://www.topikguide.com) — TOPIK test preparation resources.

## YouTube Channels

- [Talk to Me in Korean](https://www.youtube.com/@talktomeinkorean) — Grammar lessons and cultural content.
- [Go Billy Korean](https://www.youtube.com/@GoBillyKorean) — Natural-speed explanations in real settings.
- [Korean Unnie](https://www.youtube.com/@KoreanUnnie) — Practical expressions with cultural context.
- [Conversational Korean](https://www.youtube.com/@ConversationalKorean) — Real conversations for listening practice.
- [Prof Yoon's Korean Language Class](https://www.youtube.com/@ProfYoon) — Academic Korean instruction.

## Podcasts

- [Talk to Me in Korean Podcast](https://talktomeinkorean.com/audio) — Conversational Korean at multiple levels.
- [Korean Class 101 Podcast](https://www.koreanclass101.com/korean-podcast/) — Structured audio lessons.
- [TTMIK Iyagi](https://talktomeinkorean.com/curriculum/iyagi-series/) — Natural Korean conversations (intermediate+).

## Dictionaries & Reference

- [Naver Dictionary](https://dict.naver.com) — The most comprehensive Korean-English dictionary.
- [National Institute of Korean Language](https://krdict.korean.go.kr) — Official Korean dictionary with examples.
- [Wiktionary Korean](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Category:Korean_language) — Community-maintained definitions.

## Flashcards & Spaced Repetition

- [Anki](https://apps.ankiweb.net) — SRS with Korean community decks (TOPIK vocab, sentence mining).
- [Memrise](https://www.memrise.com) — Vocabulary with native speaker video clips.
- [Quizlet Korean](https://quizlet.com/subject/korean/) — Community-created Korean study sets.

## Reading Practice

- [Naver Webtoon](https://comic.naver.com) — Korean webtoons (great for casual reading).
- [News in Korean](https://www.yonhapnews.co.kr) — Yonhap News for advanced reading.
- [Namu Wiki](https://namu.wiki) — Korean Wikipedia alternative (casual writing style).
- [Korean graded readers](https://www.twochois.com) — Level-appropriate reading materials.

## Expression & Context Learning

- [Tubelang Expression Guides](https://tubelang.com/korean-expressions/en) — Deep dives into common expressions (괜찮아요, 눈치, 그냥, 대박, etc.) with real YouTube clips showing how native speakers actually use them.
- [KoreanClass101 Word of the Day](https://www.koreanclass101.com/korean-word-of-the-day/) — Daily vocabulary with examples.

## Grammar References

- [Korean Grammar Dictionary](https://koreangrammaticalforms.com) — Searchable grammar patterns.
- [TTMIK Grammar](https://talktomeinkorean.com/curriculum/) — Structured grammar by level (1-9).
- [How to Study Korean Grammar](https://www.howtostudykorean.com/unit1/) — Free comprehensive grammar guide.

- [r/learningkorean](https://www.reddit.com/r/learningkorean/) — Beginner-friendly community.
- [Korean Language Discord](https://discord.gg/korean) — Active chat community.
- [HelloTalk](https://www.hellotalk.com) — Language exchange with native Korean speakers.
